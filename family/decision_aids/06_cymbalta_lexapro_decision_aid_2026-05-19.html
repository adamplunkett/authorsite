The dose question itself has new texture: the geriatric-evidence cap is 10 mg, but Dad's historical effective dose was 30 mg. Whether 5–10 mg can do the antidepressant-background work that 30 mg historically did is open. Recommended ask of Dr. Rubin tomorrow remains sequencing: run the first 2–3 ketamine sessions on the existing duloxetine background, revisit the switch at the mid-June check-in with response data in hand. If pre-ketamine timing isn't winnable: gradual cross-titration over 6–8 weeks (not 3), start escitalopram at 5 mg and cap at 10 mg per the geriatric evidence, define explicit reversal triggers, and consider delaying the ketamine restart until the new background is stable. What OE #15 added — the ceiling-effect reframe and the severity-stratified question
  • The 40–50 mg push was above the established efficacy ceiling. Furukawa 2019 (the definitive SSRI dose-response meta-analysis) found that escitalopram efficacy plateaus at ~10–20 mg fluoxetine equivalents. SERT (serotonin transporter) occupancy data from PET imaging: 5 mg achieves ~60% occupancy, 10 mg ~64%, 20 mg ~75% — doses above 20 mg provide diminishing returns. Adverse events at 20 mg = 86% vs 66% at 10 mg vs 61% placebo. The 40–50 mg dose was operating in the flat region of the dose-response curve, where the drug had no additional antidepressant benefit to give, only mounting side effects. The escalation, in retrospect, looks like the intervention that began the recent medication-churn cycle rather than a true failure of Lexapro at the dose range that historically worked for Dad.
  • The severity-stratified question is the sharpest analytical point. Dad's historical 30 mg response was during mild-to-moderate depression. The current episode is severe (PHQ-9 = 24, worst in 40 years). OE #15 framed this directly: "If escitalopram was adequate for mild-to-moderate depression but the current episode is severe, there is no published basis for expecting [a lower dose] to control the current episode as a standalone antidepressant. The role of escitalopram in the current plan is as a background antidepressant for ketamine, not as the primary intervention — but even in that role, the question is whether it provides adequate background support." This question is sharpened, not softened, by the dose-history correction: the proposed 5–10 mg is below the dose Dad historically needed even for less-severe periods.
  • Reinstatement failure rate. Two 2025 systematic reviews (Majkic & Taylor; Bosman) document that 4–57% of patients fail to re-respond when previously-effective antidepressants are restarted after a gap. Pooled rate 16.5%. No risk-factor analysis exists for gap duration or prior dose range. Amsterdam's data on step-wise loss of effectiveness — roughly 20% reduction in response per additional antidepressant trial — compounds the same direction; Dad has had many prior trials.
  • Holper 2020 reanalysis with age as a covariate: no antidepressant provides balanced benefit (efficacy exceeding adverse events) in patients aged 70 and over, because adverse events exceed efficacy at all doses in this age group. Population-level finding, not a verdict on Dad specifically. But it argues against investing energy in switching antidepressants when no AD switch is likely to be the load-bearing intervention here.
  • 10 mg dose cap is robustly supported by general geriatric evidence if the switch proceeds. FDA-recommended maximum for elderly is 10 mg. Geriatric pharmacology: 10 mg in a 76-year-old ≈ 15 mg in a younger adult (50% increased AUC + half-life in elderly). Cardiac safety: QTcF prolongation is 4.5 ms at 10 mg vs 10.7 ms at 30 mg — relevant to Dad's LAFB + bradycardia. The 10 mg cap is below Dad's historical effective dose, however — that's the open question the dose-cap discussion has to acknowledge.
  • The 3-week cross-titration timeline is inadequate. OE #15 explicitly: a safe cross-titration in an elderly patient with anxiety-predominant TRD and withdrawal sensitivity requires a minimum of 6–8 weeks. The 3 weeks before June 8 is operationally insufficient. If the switch proceeds, the ketamine course should be delayed until the cross-titration is complete and the patient is clinically stable on the new background.
