1. Framing — Sharon's hypothesis, treated as a serious clinical question

What Sharon is right about

Sharon is right that the "wants to jump out of his skin" phenomenology is the textbook patient-language description of akathisia, and right that SSRI/SNRI-induced akathisia is real and well-documented as an entity. The DSM-5-TR (APA 2022) explicitly recognizes SSRI/SNRI-induced akathisia and notes it is "identical in phenomenology and treatment response" to antipsychotic-induced akathisia. Duloxetine specifically is listed in the FDA label adverse-reactions data as producing akathisia/psychomotor restlessness. The framing of the symptom as akathisia is reasonable, and the impulse to look at duloxetine first is reasonable.

Where the evidence as we currently understand it doesn't yet support the attribution

1. Time-course mismatch. The DSM-5-TR specifies that medication-induced acute akathisia "typically occur[s] within 4 weeks of initiating or increasing the dose." The published SSRI/SNRI akathisia literature (Akgoz 2024, Koliscak 2009) samples patients during the 4–8 week initiation window. Dad has been at stable 60 mg AM since the titration completed on 11/03/2025 — ~6.5 months of stable dosing without titration. De novo emergence of SNRI-induced akathisia after ~6 months of stable dosing without a dose change is not a well-documented entity in the published literature.

2. The two 24-hour taper attempts didn't test the hypothesis. Duloxetine has a ~12-hour half-life, but downstream receptor adaptation takes days-to-weeks. A sustained reduction held for at least 7–14 days at a reduced dose, with daily BARS (Barnes Akathisia Rating Scale) scoring, would be needed to generate interpretable data. Each of the 5/12 and 5/21 attempts was reversed within 24 hours when acute anxiety surged — which is the rebound anxiety pattern, not evidence that Cymbalta is causing akathisia.

3. Cleaner temporal alternatives exist for the motor phenotype. The chart-documented shaking episodes (5/17 AM, 5/22 PM, 5/23 AM) have a cleaner temporal link to the single-night doxepin 12 mg titration on 5/16 — the only medication change that night. Nordoxepin (31-hour half-life) remains meaningfully on board through ~5/26.

2. The phenotype distinction matters

Shaking ≠ akathisia (per DSM-5-TR definitions)

Akathisia's motor component per DSM-5-TR is "purposeless movements that relieve subjective tension" — fidgeting, rocking, pacing, weight-shifting. It is goal-directed in service of relieving inner restlessness.

Tremor is rhythmic, involuntary oscillation — neither purposeful nor responsive to inner state.

Mom's characterization of Dad's episodes — "episodic, brief, like shaking when cold" — sounds more consistent with tremor or myoclonus than with the stereotyped motor component of akathisia. This raises the real possibility that the shaking and the inner restlessness ("wants to jump out of his skin") are two distinct symptoms with potentially different etiologies, which would significantly complicate a single-agent attribution.

Highest-yield diagnostic step on this dimension: a formal Barnes Akathisia Rating Scale assessment by a clinician — separates the inner-restlessness component (BARS captures it) from the motor phenomenology, and provides a quantitative baseline for later comparison.

3. The differential, ranked by Bayesian probability

  1. Endogenous agitated depression / mixed-features TRD. MODERATE
    PHQ-9 = 24 is the worst in 40 years. Agitated depression characteristically worsens with antidepressants, which fits Dad's multi-class activation-intolerance pattern. The "jump out of skin" phenomenology is well-described in agitated depression (Alhau 2025), phenomenologically indistinguishable from akathisia per DSM-5-TR. This is the most likely dominant contributor.
  2. Prodromal Lewy-spectrum substrate (not the trigger, but the underlying vulnerability). MODERATE TO ROBUST
    RBD 8.5 years + gait abnormality + balance findings + chronic constipation + peripheral neuropathy = high prior probability of synucleinopathy per Fereshtehnejad 2019, Joza 2023, McKeith 2020 prodromal-DLB research criteria. Motor signs accelerate 3–5 years before phenoconversion. The cross-class activation-intolerance pattern (6 documented agents) likely reflects this substrate — a dopaminergically compromised basal ganglia produces movement-disorder phenomena in response to many pharmacological interventions that would be tolerated by neurotypical patients.
  3. Doxepin-induced tremor + nordoxepin tail. MODERATE
    For the shaking phenotype specifically, the temporal correlation is the cleanest of any candidate: first acute shaking 5/17 AM directly followed the single-night 12 mg doxepin titration on 5/16. Nordoxepin's 31-hour half-life means accumulated metabolite remains meaningfully present through ~5/26. Less explanatory for the inner-restlessness component.
  4. Severe sleep deprivation as amplifier. MODERATE
    <3 hours/night for several days produces irritability, agitation, and a restless-distress phenotype that mimics akathisia. Reversible with sleep restoration; compounds whatever else is contributing.
  5. Post-olanzapine + concurrent lithium withdrawal (compound). SPARSE
    Olanzapine + lithium simultaneously discontinued 4/21/2026 — current cluster is ~32 days out. The olanzapine exposure was brief (12 days, ~6 at 5 mg). Published literature on withdrawal akathisia is dominated by longer exposures; classical withdrawal akathisia at 32 days post a 12-day exposure sits at the outer edge of plausibility. The near-panic-attack rebound on 4/22 (first full day off) confirms Dad did experience acute withdrawal phenomenology.
  6. Duloxetine itself — Sharon's hypothesis on the current evidence. SPARSE TO ABSENT (current evidence)
    Dose stable at 60 mg since 11/3/2025. De novo emergence at ~6.5 months on stable dose without titration is not documented in the published SSRI/SNRI akathisia literature. This ranking would shift substantially upward under any of the five scenarios in Section 4.
  7. Residual drug-induced tremor from prior agents (lithium, brexpiprazole, pramipexole, olanzapine). SPARSE
    Lithium-induced tremor specifically can persist weeks-to-months post-discontinuation. May be re-emerging on the underlying substrate.
  8. RBD exacerbation / dream-enactment. SPARSE
    Less consistent with Mom's characterization (brief, not associated with dream recall), but on the list given Dad's 8.5-year formal RBD.

4. What would shift weight toward Sharon's Cymbalta hypothesis

Five scenarios — some involving historical revision, some involving mechanistic reframing, some involving direct testing — that would legitimately increase the posterior probability for duloxetine as the dominant cause. Listed in roughly increasing order of how actionable they are.

Scenario 1 — Retrospective discovery of subtle akathisia dating to Cymbalta initiation/titration (9/30/2025 – 11/3/2025)
Probability shift if confirmed: MODERATE ↑  ·  Mechanism evidence: MODERATE (canonical temporal criterion)

This is the most straightforward scenario and the one Adam directly asked about. If targeted history-taking with Dad and Mom revealed that Dad experienced low-grade inner restlessness, leg discomfort, or a subtle "can't get comfortable" sensation beginning within the first 4 weeks of Cymbalta initiation (started 9/30/2025) or titration (completed 11/3/2025), this would satisfy the DSM-5-TR canonical temporal criterion for medication-induced acute akathisia.

How to test it: Targeted history with Dad ("In November or early December last year, when you were ramping up on the new Cymbalta, did you notice anything like leg discomfort, wanting to shift around, or feeling like you couldn't get comfortable?") + independent corroboration from Mom (any restlessness behaviors she recalls from that window) + chart review of any 11/2025–12/2025 follow-up visits for restlessness complaints.

Methodological caveat: retrospective patient self-report of subtle symptoms 6 months later is highly susceptible to recall bias, especially in a patient with PHQ-9 = 24 and high current distress. The published literature on SSRI/SNRI-induced movement disorders identifies retrospective self-report as a known methodological limitation (Heinz 2022). A patient in acute distress who is told "your Cymbalta might be causing this" may retrospectively reinterpret prior nonspecific discomfort as early akathisia. Independent corroboration would be needed to make this finding credible.

Scenario 2 — The "unmasking" hypothesis: olanzapine was suppressing Cymbalta-induced akathisia
Probability shift if confirmed: MODERATE ↑  ·  Mechanism evidence: ABSENT as documented entity (pharmacologically plausible)

This is the most intellectually interesting scenario and the one that could reframe the timeline entirely. The logic: olanzapine's 5-HT2A antagonism and mild D2 blockade are precisely the pharmacological properties that treat akathisia — mirtazapine and 5-HT2A antagonists are among the most effective akathisia treatments (Factor 2019, Gerolymos 2024). If Cymbalta was producing low-grade akathisia that was being pharmacologically suppressed by concurrent olanzapine (started 4/9/2026), then olanzapine discontinuation on 4/21 would unmask the underlying SNRI-induced akathisia — and the symptom emergence ~26 days later (first shaking 5/17) could represent the gradual loss of that suppressive effect as the system re-equilibrates.

How to test it: Retrospective comparison of Dad's symptom burden during the 12-day olanzapine trial (4/9–4/21) vs. the weeks before it and the weeks after. If Dad was notably calmer or less restless during the olanzapine window, that would be a meaningful signal in favor of this hypothesis. The 4/22 near-panic-attack (first full day off olanzapine) could be reinterpreted as the first signal of unmasked akathisia rather than pure withdrawal.

Mechanism caveat: Olanzapine-treats-SNRI-akathisia is pharmacologically coherent, but the specific "unmasking on discontinuation" scenario is absent from the published literature as a documented entity. It would also still attribute the underlying cause to Cymbalta, which requires explaining why Cymbalta produced akathisia after ~5–6 months of stable dosing — returning to Scenario 1.

Scenario 3 — Pharmacokinetic shift: Cymbalta levels rising without dose change [HIGHEST-YIELD TEST]
Probability shift if confirmed: LARGE ↑↑  ·  Mechanism evidence: ROBUST  ·  Directly testable today

A previously tolerated stable dose could become effectively supratherapeutic if Cymbalta clearance has decreased. Duloxetine is metabolized primarily by CYP1A2 and CYP2D6 (Knadler 2011). Several mechanisms could raise plasma levels without a dose change:

  • CYP2D6 poor metabolizer status — CYP2D6 PMs have ~95% higher duloxetine concentration-to-dose ratios; patients ≥65 years have ~56% higher ratios than younger patients (Hole 2024). If Dad is a CYP2D6 PM, his effective Cymbalta exposure at 60 mg could be equivalent to ~120 mg in a younger normal metabolizer.
  • New CYP1A2 inhibitor — fluvoxamine co-administration increases duloxetine AUC by ~460%; any new ciprofloxacin, cimetidine, or certain herbal supplements could matter.
  • Smoking cessation — smoking induces CYP1A2 and reduces duloxetine bioavailability by ~30%; recent cessation would raise levels. (Dad is not a recent quitter, but worth confirming.)
  • Hepatic function decline — even modest impairment can produce a 5-fold AUC increase in moderate hepatic impairment per FDA label.
  • Generic manufacturer switch — different generic formulations can vary within the FDA-permitted ±20% bioavailability range; in a CYP2D6 PM elderly patient this could be clinically meaningful.

How to test it: A duloxetine serum trough level (drawn before the morning dose) is the single most informative test for this entire question. Reference range at steady state is typically 30–120 ng/mL. If Dad's level is disproportionately high for 60 mg, this would substantially strengthen Sharon's attribution and the path forward becomes concrete (dose reduction targeted to a specific level rather than blind taper). If the level is within normal range, the PK-shift hypothesis is excluded.

CYP2D6 pharmacogenomic testing is the complementary test — it would reveal if Dad is a poor or intermediate metabolizer, providing the mechanistic explanation if the level is high. Many large reference labs (Quest, LabCorp, Mayo Reference) offer both tests; turnaround is typically 1–2 weeks for genotyping, 2–5 business days for the serum level.

Why this is the load-bearing recommendation: it is the only scenario in which a single clinical action can produce a clear, interpretable answer within the ECT bridge window without requiring a destabilizing taper attempt.

Scenario 4 — Lewy-spectrum threshold lowering: Cymbalta as proximate trigger on a progressively vulnerable substrate
Probability shift if confirmed: MODERATE ↑  ·  Mechanism evidence: MODERATE (DLB sensitivity well-documented)  ·  Reframes as dual causation

This is the most important reframing. In DLB, progressive loss of nigrostriatal dopaminergic neurons creates a state where medications previously tolerated become intolerable — not because the drug changed, but because the brain's capacity to compensate has declined (Piggott 1998, Baskys 2004, McKeith 2005). DLB patients show a characteristic failure to upregulate D2 receptors in response to dopaminergic challenge, which is the proposed mechanism for their severe neuroleptic sensitivity. Approximately 50% of DLB patients experience severe adverse reactions to antipsychotics, and this sensitivity extends beyond antipsychotics to any agent that perturbs dopaminergic-serotonergic balance.

If Dad's prodromal synucleinopathy has progressed over the past 6 months (which is expected — motor signs accelerate ~3 years before phenoconversion), then Cymbalta's noradrenergic and serotonergic effects could now be producing akathisia on a substrate that previously compensated. This is the scenario where Sharon's attribution and the Lewy-spectrum attribution are not mutually exclusive — Cymbalta could be the proximate trigger on a progressively vulnerable substrate.

How to test it: The DAT-SPECT (pending at U-M Movement Disorders) would be pivotal. If positive, it reframes the entire medication-intolerance history. The multi-class activation-intolerance pattern should also show a temporal progression — agents tolerated earlier in 2025 becoming intolerable later, consistent with declining neurological reserve. The symptom cluster should be progressive rather than abruptly tied to one medication change.

Scenario 5 — Tardive akathisia from SNRI (chronic-exposure variant)
Probability shift if confirmed: MINIMAL  ·  Mechanism evidence: ABSENT (not documented with SNRIs)

Tardive akathisia is a recognized entity with antipsychotics — phenomenologically identical to acute akathisia but emerging during chronic stable dosing and, critically, worsening rather than improving when the causative agent is removed (Factor 2019, Frei 2018). If Cymbalta were producing tardive akathisia, the failed taper attempts could be reinterpreted as consistent with this — though the worsening was characterized as "anxiety" rather than akathisia specifically. Tardive akathisia is fundamentally a D2-receptor-blocking-agent phenomenon and has not been documented with SNRIs in the published literature. Very low probability without a novel mechanism not supported by current evidence.

5. What NOT to do — propranolol contraindication + don't push another Cymbalta taper

PROPRANOLOL IS CONTRAINDICATED IN DAD

Earlier framing in family-thread discussion floated propranolol 10–20 mg TID as first-line akathisia treatment. That recommendation does not apply to Dad and should be withdrawn.

Propranolol is contraindicated in sinus bradycardia per the FDA label. Dad's baseline HR is 49 bpm (frank sinus bradycardia, documented at the 12/2024 PCP wellness visit). Combined with LAFB and PR 214 ms (borderline 1° AV block), propranolol carries significant risk of hemodynamically significant bradycardia or progression of conduction disease. The AHA scientific statement on drug-induced arrhythmias (Tisdale 2020) advises that drugs inhibiting sinus or AV node function "should be avoided in patients with preexisting dysfunction." Propranolol should not be used in Dad without cardiology clearance and a refreshed EKG — and even then would require extreme caution.

DO NOT push another Cymbalta taper before ECT

The two single-day taper attempts (5/12, 5/21) each produced acute anxiety crisis that required reinstatement within 24 hours. Continuing to attempt the taper without a sustained ≥7–14 day reduction-and-hold pattern (with daily BARS scoring) will produce more rebound anxiety crises without testing the akathisia-causation hypothesis. The published evidence supports holding Cymbalta steady at 60 mg through ECT initiation (PRIDE study; Pluijms 2021 meta-analysis on SNRI continuation through ECT). Any decision about Cymbalta's long-term role should be deferred to the U-M ECT team.

Key findingConfirms efficacy of benzodiazepines, mirtazapine, and beta-blockers for antipsychotic-induced akathisia. Clonazepam specifically supported among benzodiazepines for chronic akathisia management.
How it appliesIndependent corroboration of the clonazepam recommendation; also confirms no treatment is differentially diagnostic.
Tier 2 — Duloxetine pharmacology and pharmacokinetics (load-bearing for Scenario 3)
Knadler MP, Lobo E, Chappell J, Bergstrom R. Duloxetine: Clinical Pharmacokinetics and Drug Interactions.
Clin Pharmacokinet 2011;50(5):281-94. doi:10.2165/11539240-000000000-00000.
Key findingDuloxetine metabolized primarily by CYP1A2 and CYP2D6. Fluvoxamine (CYP1A2 inhibitor) increases duloxetine AUC by ~460%. Smoking induces CYP1A2 and reduces duloxetine bioavailability ~30%. Hepatic impairment substantially increases AUC.
How it appliesMechanistic basis for Scenario 3 (PK shift). Multiple plausible pathways for a stable dose to become supratherapeutic without a dose change — testable via serum level and CYP2D6 genotyping.
Hole K, Gangsø S, Jensstuen ÅT et al. Effect of CYP2D6 Genotype on Duloxetine Serum Concentration.
Basic Clin Pharmacol Toxicol 2024;134(1):186-192. doi:10.1111/bcpt.13954.
Key findingCYP2D6 poor metabolizers have ~95% higher duloxetine concentration-to-dose ratios than normal metabolizers. Patients ≥65 years show ~56% higher ratios than younger patients.
How it appliesQuantifies the magnitude of effect for the PK-shift hypothesis. A 76-year-old CYP2D6 PM at 60 mg could plausibly have exposure equivalent to ~120 mg in a younger normal metabolizer. This is why a serum level + genotype is the highest-yield single test.
Tier 3 — Prodromal Lewy-spectrum framework (load-bearing for Scenario 4)
Fereshtehnejad SM, Yao C, Pelletier A et al. Evolution of Prodromal Parkinson's Disease and Dementia With Lewy Bodies: A Prospective Study.
Brain 2019;142(7):2051-2067. doi:10.1093/brain/awz111.
Key findingProspective cohort tracking prodromal phenoconversion. Motor signs accelerate ~3 years before phenoconversion; non-motor prodromal features (RBD, autonomic, olfactory) present 5–9 years earlier. RBD ≥5 years carries high prior probability of synucleinopathy.
How it appliesEstablishes the prior probability that Dad's 8.5-year RBD + gait/balance/autonomic findings represent prodromal synucleinopathy. Anchors Scenario 4: progressive substrate vulnerability could be lowering the threshold for medication-induced phenomena.
Joza S, Hu MT, Jung KY et al. Progression of Clinical Markers in Prodromal Parkinson's Disease and Dementia With Lewy Bodies: A Multicentre Study.
Brain 2023;146(8):3258-3272. doi:10.1093/brain/awad072.
Key findingConfirms acceleration of motor and cognitive markers in the 3-year window before phenoconversion across multiple international cohorts.
How it appliesIndependent corroboration of the prodromal-progression framework. Strengthens the rationale for prioritizing the U-M Movement Disorders workup + DAT-SPECT.
McKeith IG, Ferman TJ, Thomas AJ et al. Research Criteria for the Diagnosis of Prodromal Dementia With Lewy Bodies.
Neurology 2020;94(17):743-755. doi:10.1212/WNL.0000000000009323.
Key findingFormal research criteria for prodromal DLB. Includes depression, anxiety, delirium-like fluctuations, RBD, and autonomic dysfunction as recognized prodromal features.
How it appliesAnchors the framing that Dad's depression and anxiety, alongside the RBD and autonomic features, can themselves be prodromal-DLB features — not just psychiatric comorbidity.
Piggott MA, Perry EK, Marshall EF et al. Nigrostriatal Dopaminergic Activities in DLB in Relation to Neuroleptic Sensitivity: Comparisons With Parkinson's Disease.
Biol Psychiatry 1998;44(8):765-74. doi:10.1016/s0006-3223(98)00127-9.
Key findingDLB patients show failure to upregulate D2 receptors in response to dopaminergic challenge — the proposed mechanism for severe neuroleptic sensitivity. ~50% of DLB patients experience severe adverse reactions to antipsychotics; sensitivity extends beyond antipsychotics to agents that perturb dopaminergic-serotonergic balance.
How it appliesMechanistic basis for Scenario 4. Explains why a multi-class activation-intolerance pattern across antipsychotics, antidepressants, and dopaminergics could reflect underlying substrate vulnerability rather than per-drug intolerance.
Kaila LV, Ikeda M, Sultana J et al. The Evolving Therapeutic Landscape of Dementia With Lewy Bodies.
Lancet Neurol 2025;24(12):1038-1052. doi:10.1016/S1474-4422(25)00323-0.
Key findingCurrent state of the art on DLB therapeutics. Cholinesterase inhibitors strongest evidence for neuropsychiatric symptoms; SSRIs/SNRIs acceptable for depression; clonazepam for RBD; quetiapine cautiously usable for sleep/neuropsychiatric symptoms at low doses; tricyclics and high-D2-affinity antipsychotics avoided.
How it appliesSupports the clonazepam recommendation and the cautious low-dose quetiapine framing. Also informs the medium-term plan for Dad if Lewy-spectrum is confirmed.
Tier 4 — Cardiac safety and propranolol contraindication
Tisdale JE, Chung MK, Campbell KB et al. Drug-Induced Arrhythmias: A Scientific Statement From the American Heart Association.
Circulation 2020;142(15):e214-e233. doi:10.1161/CIR.0000000000000905.
Key findingDrugs inhibiting sinus or AV node function "should be avoided in patients with preexisting dysfunction." Propranolol FDA label contraindicates sinus bradycardia. AV nodal blockade additive with conduction abnormalities.
How it appliesReverses earlier family-thread floats about propranolol for akathisia. Dad's HR 49 + LAFB + PR 214 ms triplicate-stacks the contraindication. Propranolol is off the table without cardiology clearance.
Tier 5 — ECT in psychomotor-agitated depression (load-bearing for primary plan)
van Diermen L, Vanmarcke S, Walther S et al. Can Psychomotor Disturbance Predict ECT Outcome in Depression?
J Psychiatr Res 2019;117:122-128. doi:10.1016/j.jpsychires.2019.07.009.
Key findingPsychomotor disturbance (agitation OR retardation) is a strong predictor of ECT response. OR 4.9 for response in CORE-defined melancholic depression with documented psychomotor disturbance.
How it appliesDad's psychomotor agitation predicts good ECT response. Strong independent reason for ECT to be the primary plan regardless of how the akathisia differential resolves.
Suzuki A, Kobayashi R, Shirata T et al. Case Report: Changes in Regional Cerebral Blood Flow in Chronic Akathisia of a Depressed Patient Before and After ECT.
Front Psychiatry 2021;12:728265. doi:10.3389/fpsyt.2021.728265.
Key findingDocumented case of chronic akathisia resolving with ECT, with imaging-correlated basal ganglia neuroplasticity changes.
How it appliesSupports ECT as both treatment and diagnostic probe — rapid resolution of restlessness phenotype following ECT initiation would support endogenous agitated depression as dominant contributor (though it doesn't definitively exclude iatrogenic components).
Tier 6 — Antipsychotic withdrawal phenomena (for Scenarios involving olanzapine d/c)
Brandt L, Bschor T, Henssler J et al. Antipsychotic Withdrawal Symptoms: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis.
Front Psychiatry 2020;11:569912. doi:10.3389/fpsyt.2020.569912.
Key findingWithdrawal symptoms occur in ~53% of patients after antipsychotic discontinuation. Literature dominated by longer-exposure cases; brief-exposure withdrawal phenomenology less well-characterized.
How it appliesConstrains the post-olanzapine-withdrawal hypothesis (differential #5): Dad's olanzapine exposure was 12 days, ~6 at full 5 mg dose — at the brief end of where the literature applies.
Cosci F, Chouinard G. Acute and Persistent Withdrawal Syndromes Following Discontinuation of Psychotropic Medications.
Psychother Psychosom 2020;89(5):283-306. doi:10.1159/000506868.
Key findingComprehensive review of withdrawal syndromes across psychotropic classes. Lithium withdrawal: anxiety, irritability, mood instability, typically within 1–2 weeks. Atypical antipsychotic withdrawal: insomnia, anxiety, movement disturbances. Most withdrawal phenomena emerge within 1–2 weeks.
How it appliesPlaces the 4/22 near-panic-attack rebound (first full day off olanzapine) within the expected window. Frames the ~32-day delay to current symptom cluster as outside the typical withdrawal-phenomenology window for a brief exposure.
Tier 7 — Agitated depression and SNRI continuation through ECT
Alhau R, Rimawi M, Zubrzycka I, Mahgoub Y. Agitated Depression and the Potential Response to GABA Agonist: A Case Series and Literature Review.
J Clin Psychopharmacol 2025;45(1):32-36. doi:10.1097/JCP.0000000000001935.
Key findingAgitated depression characteristically worsens with antidepressants. "Jump out of skin" phenomenology recognized as agitated-depression presentation. GABAergic augmentation (benzodiazepine, gabapentinoid) can ameliorate.
How it appliesReinforces the endogenous-agitated-depression candidate as the most likely dominant contributor and frames clonazepam (Section 6, item 5) as both a sleep aid and a candidate for symptomatic relief of the restlessness phenotype.

9. Evidence quality summary

What the evidence actually supports

Robust: Propranolol contraindication in bradycardia (FDA label + Tisdale 2020 AHA statement). Duloxetine PK and CYP-mediated interaction profile (Knadler 2011). Prodromal DLB phenoconversion framework (Fereshtehnejad 2019, Joza 2023). ECT efficacy in psychomotor-agitated depression (van Diermen 2019, OR 4.9).

Moderate: SSRI/SNRI initiation akathisia (Akgoz 2024, Koliscak 2009). CYP2D6 PM status effect on duloxetine exposure (Hole 2024). DLB neuroleptic sensitivity mechanism (Piggott 1998). Endogenous agitated depression worsening with antidepressants (Alhau 2025). Clonazepam for RBD and for akathisia (Gerolymos 2024, Gambolò 2024, McKeith 2005, Taylor 2020).

Sparse: Withdrawal akathisia 30+ days post brief antipsychotic exposure. SNRI akathisia de novo on chronic stable dosing without titration. Differential-diagnostic value of treatment response (propranolol response not differentially diagnostic). Olanzapine-suppressing-SNRI-akathisia as documented entity (pharmacologically plausible per Factor 2019 but not directly documented).

Absent: Validated differential checklist for akathisia vs. agitated depression vs. prodromal-Lewy restlessness. Compound olanzapine + lithium withdrawal phenomenology as a studied entity. Tardive akathisia from SNRIs (D2-blocker phenomenon only per Frei 2018).
