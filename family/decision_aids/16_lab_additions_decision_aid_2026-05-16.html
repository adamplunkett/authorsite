Ranked lab additions for next PCP draw
Test Priority Rationale in his profile
AM cortisol + ACTH
(8 AM fasting)		 Essential — high-yield rule-out Inexpensive, high-consequence rule-out for adrenal insufficiency. The symptom triad of chronic fatigue (R53.82) + unintentional weight loss (R63.4) + decreased appetite (R63.0) overlaps with adrenal insufficiency presentation. Morning cortisol >10 µg/dL very likely excludes it; <3 µg/dL is highly suggestive. Pre-test probability is low (no exogenous glucocorticoid exposure) but the cost is low and the consequence of a missed diagnosis is high.
Repeat AM total T (fasting) + LH + FSH + prolactin Essential — hypogonadism characterization Confirmed biochemical hypogonadism on 5/4. Repeat morning total T is mandatory (~30% of men with initially low T normalize on retest). LH/FSH distinguish primary (testicular) from secondary (pituitary/hypothalamic) hypogonadism. Prolactin is especially important given the recent olanzapine exposure — antipsychotic-induced hyperprolactinemia can suppress gonadotropins and lower T, typically resolving within 4–6 weeks of discontinuation (so prolactin drawn now is well-timed to catch any residual elevation). Per Endocrine Society + AUA guidelines.
Iron panel — ferritin + TSAT + CRP
(serum iron + TIBC also acceptable)		 Essential — already on family's list Already in the clinical brief's proposed adds; not yet done. Ferritin + TSAT is the AGA-recommended starting panel. CRP serves double duty — interpretive context for ferritin (any inflammation can falsely elevate it) plus weight-loss workup baseline. Ferritin alone can mislead in elderly inflammation; pair it with CRP. Soluble transferrin receptor (sTfR) reserved for ambiguous cases (ferritin 30–100 with elevated CRP). Even with normal CBC, early iron deficiency presents with low ferritin before MCV/RDW change — Hgb 14.3 is reassuring but doesn't rule out the question.
Homocysteine + folate Essential — completes B12 functional picture The complementary B12 functional marker — paired with the existing MMA (0.46, borderline elevated), homocysteine distinguishes mechanism: both elevated → B12 deficiency confirmed; MMA elevated + homocysteine normal → B12 specific; MMA normal + homocysteine elevated → folate deficiency or renal. Folate drawn simultaneously — folate deficiency independently contributes to depression and cognitive symptoms and is inexpensive to check. Holotranscobalamin is optional given MMA is already done.
25(OH) Vitamin D Essential — already on family's list Already in the clinical brief's proposed adds; not yet done. The 2024 Endocrine Society guideline suggests empiric vitamin D supplementation (800–1000 IU/day) for all adults ≥75 regardless of testing, but for patients with depression + cognitive complaints + fall-risk picture, measurement guides dosing intensity. Decision-relevant thresholds: <20 ng/mL warrants pharmacologic supplementation; <30 ng/mL warrants OTC supplementation; ≥30 ng/mL adequate.
SPEP + LDH Essential — weight loss workup standards Per AAFP unintentional-weight-loss guideline. SPEP (serum protein electrophoresis) screens for monoclonal gammopathies (MGUS, multiple myeloma, Waldenström's, AL amyloidosis) — these can present with fatigue, weight loss, neuropathy, and cognitive decline in elderly men. LDH is a nonspecific but inexpensive marker included in standard weight-loss/malignancy screening. These are notable omissions from the labs done so far.
Urinalysis Essential — weight loss workup Standard component of the AAFP unintentional-weight-loss workup. Also relevant ~8 weeks post-Aquablation (any residual hematuria, signs of UTI, glycosuria given pre-diabetes), and could pick up signs of occult renal or urologic process. Inexpensive, no patient burden.
FIT (fecal immunochemical test)
(at-home collection)		 Essential — colon adenoma surveillance Dad has colon adenoma (D12.6) on his problem list since 2016 and family history of colon cancer (Z80.0). FIT is the modern fecal occult blood test for colorectal screening in patients with adenoma history. Particularly important to have on file before any iron-panel results — if iron studies come back low, colonoscopy becomes more urgent and a positive FIT would accelerate the timeline. Independent of the iron question, the family-history + adenoma combination makes this overdue.
PSA + lipid panel
(baselines)		 Essential — baselines PSA is required as a baseline before any TRT consideration (which depends on the hypogonadism workup direction) and is a standard add at this age. Lipid panel is standard statin-monitoring on rosuvastatin 20 mg — likely overdue. Both are routine, low-cost adds.
NT-proBNP Essential — occult heart failure rule-out Given the bradycardia (HR 57) + LAFB + chronic fatigue + functional decline + weight loss picture, NT-proBNP is the right rule-out for occult heart failure as a contributor. In patients >75, NT-proBNP <125 pg/mL effectively excludes heart failure; rule-in threshold is 1,800 pg/mL. Normal result closes off that piece of the differential cleanly. If elevated, would prompt echocardiogram referral.
ESR
(add to CRP)		 Consider — moderate add to CRP Adds modestly to CRP for the inflammation/weight-loss workup. Different time-course than CRP (slower to rise and fall). Worth adding if it's a low-effort include; CRP alone is sufficient if the draw is being kept focused.
Estradiol Consider — if symptomatic Part of full hypogonadism workup if breast symptoms or gynecomastia present; otherwise lower priority. Add if endocrine workup direction warrants.
Plasma p-Tau217, NfL, GFAP, Aβ42/40 ratio Defer to U-M Cognitive Disorders Plasma p-Tau217 is the AAIC-2025 Core 1 blood biomarker for Alzheimer pathology. But the cognitive picture isn't yet formally characterized (depression-related cognitive symptoms vs. medication effects vs. neurodegenerative process are all on the differential), and pre-test probability matters for interpretation. Let U-M Cognitive Disorders coordinate these in the context of their full evaluation — neuropsychological testing, imaging, clinical interview — rather than ordering at the PCP level.
Skin α-synuclein SAA
(Syn-One)		 Defer to U-M Movement Disorders Sensitivity 75–87%, specificity ~100% for detecting synucleinopathy in patients with RBD. FDA Breakthrough Device Designation 10/2025; not yet 510(k) cleared. Best coordinated by U-M Movement Disorders alongside their clinical exam, DaTscan, and other assessments — they can integrate the result properly and navigate insurance/availability questions.
Repeat MMA (~August 2026) Defer — recheck at 3 months To assess B12 supplementation response. MMA typically normalizes within 6–12 weeks of adequate B12 supplementation; ~15–25% of elderly with moderately poor B12 status show persistent elevation at 8 weeks of 500 µg/day. Recheck around August 2026. Non-normalizing MMA would prompt anti-intrinsic factor antibody testing and possible switch to parenteral B12.
Free T3 + reverse T3 Skip — low yield with normal TSH TSH 1.05 and free T4 0.94 are solidly normal. The American Thyroid Association explicitly notes that reverse T3 "adds little" to clinical practice with no evidence to support its use in guiding therapy. Low yield here.
Heavy metals panel
(lead, mercury, arsenic)		 Skip unless exposure history Population-level associations between heavy metals and cognitive decline exist, but as individual diagnostic tests these are low-yield without a specific exposure history (occupational, hobby, lead paint, fish consumption, well water). AAFP dementia guidelines list this as "consider based on clinical circumstances" rather than routine. Skip unless targeted history reveals plausible exposure.
Holotranscobalamin (active B12) Skip — redundant More sensitive first-line B12 marker than serum B12, but adds limited value when MMA is already done and B12 supplementation has started. Skip.
Equilibrium dialysis free T Skip — calculated values clear Gold standard for free T, but not necessary when calculated free T (Vermeulen equation) with known SHBG and albumin is already clearly low. Could be added as confirmatory if clinical-laboratory discordance arises, but not needed now.
Pituitary MRI Skip — only if LH/FSH point that way Indicated only if total T <150 or if LH/FSH suggest secondary hypogonadism with concerning features. Wait for the LH/FSH results before considering this.
