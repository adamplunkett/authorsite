Recommendation

The cleanest version of Dr. R's plan — given the ketamine infusion tomorrow morning — is to skip Seroquel tonight, continue doxepin at 6 mg HS, loop the ketamine clinic in first thing tomorrow, and revisit the Seroquel timing after tomorrow's session. Dr. R's pharmacological framing is supported by the evidence: at 25–50 mg, quetiapine is genuinely different from the atypicals Dad has reacted to (D2 occupancy in the single-digit-percent range; H1 sedation dominates; akathisia risk the lowest of the second-generation antipsychotics). The 11/2025 single fill was at 50 mg with no documented reason for stopping — worth asking Dr. R what happened. Three things shift the calculus for tonight specifically: (1) tomorrow's IV ketamine session — no published data exist on quetiapine + ketamine, the mechanistic prior is "attenuation, not augmentation" via 5-HT2A antagonism, and standard ketamine-clinic protocols ask about antipsychotic timing around session days; (2) the AM dose has no published evidence base in elderly anxiety and would put peak quetiapine levels right at infusion time tomorrow; (3) the doxepin reduction from 12 → 6 mg may be most of the answer — TCAs in older adults can produce paradoxical activation during rapid uptitration, so backing off the doxepin alone may resolve last night's episode without adding anything. If Seroquel does proceed after the ketamine session: start at 12.5 mg HS only (omit the AM dose entirely until daytime anxiety is shown to be the limiting symptom), fresh ECG within the first week given LAFB + bradycardia + the 1-year-old baseline, orthostatic vitals the next morning, and clear stopping rules for any new motor symptom. The operative plan otherwise — watchful waiting on doxepin 6 mg, trazodone PRN as the next step if needed, ketamine doing the heavy lifting — is well-shaped for this moment and should stay the comparator.

The evidence
What supports Dr. R's plan What argues for the modified version — in Dad's specific profile
"Acts differently from the other atypicals" is supported by the receptor data. PET imaging studies show that at 25 mg, quetiapine produces 56–81% H1 receptor occupancy in the cortex (Sato 2015) while D2 occupancy remains in the single-digit-percent range at trough. The Lako 2013 meta-analysis estimated quetiapine's maximum achievable D2 occupancy at only 49% (vs. ~70–80% for olanzapine, aripiprazole, brexpiprazole at their typical doses). The binding hierarchy (H1 Ki 4.4 nM >> α1b 14.6 nM >> 5-HT2A 38 nM >> D2 626 nM) means at 25–50 mg the dominant effects are sedation and α1 blockade, not D2 antagonism. Dr. R's framing is pharmacologically accurate. Tomorrow's IV ketamine session is the immediate timing constraint. There are no published trials of quetiapine + IV ketamine for TRD. The benzodiazepine-attenuation literature (Andrashko 2020; Feeney 2022) doesn't translate mechanistically — quetiapine isn't a GABA agent. But the closer mechanistic concern is quetiapine's potent 5-HT2A antagonism, which sits on the same downstream pathway ketamine uses for synaptogenesis and plasticity. The direction of the prior is "attenuation or neutral," not "augmentation." Standard ketamine-clinic protocols ask about antipsychotic timing around session days regardless. With infusion ~10–12 hours after a bedtime dose, parent drug would still be at ~25% of peak and norquetiapine substantial. The AM dose tomorrow before infusion is the worst-case timing.
Quetiapine is the safest atypical for possible Lewy-spectrum disease. Per the DLB consensus literature (Aarsland 2005; McKeith 2017 DLB Consortium; NICE NG97; MDS guidance on PD psychosis): quetiapine has the lowest D2 occupancy among the "full" atypicals and is the preferred choice when an antipsychotic is required in DLB or PD psychosis. This is meaningfully different from the prior agents Dad reacted to — aripiprazole and brexpiprazole are D2 partial agonists with high D2 affinity; olanzapine has substantially higher D2 affinity than quetiapine. If an antipsychotic must be on board during the neurology workup, quetiapine is the right choice. The AM dose has no published evidence base. The GAD trial in older adults (Mezhebovsky 2013) used quetiapine XR once daily in the evening, not a split HS + AM schedule. No published data support 6.25–12.5 mg AM quetiapine for anxiety in elderly patients. At that dose the dominant effect is H1-mediated sedation (the same effect that helps sleep at night), with minimal NET occupancy from norquetiapine since the metabolite requires higher parent-drug doses (~150 mg) for meaningful noradrenergic activity. Daytime sedation, orthostasis, and cognitive blunting are the likely effects — without a clear anxiolytic mechanism at this dose. Dad still drives, bikes, plays disc golf, and reads — the daytime burden matters.
Akathisia and EPS risk are the lowest among second-generation antipsychotics. A dose-response meta-analysis (Wu 2023) found quetiapine carried "negligible risks for akathisia across examined doses (flat curve)." Annual akathisia incidence is ~4% (vs. ~20% for other SGAs, 50% for haloperidol). In the MDD adjunctive therapy trial, akathisia incidence was 1.5% at 300 mg. The activation-intolerance concern from prior agents is mechanistically less likely to transfer to quetiapine, because the receptors that produce activation on aripiprazole/brexpiprazole/olanzapine (D2) and on mirtazapine (noradrenergic alpha-2 disinhibition) are not the dominant receptors at quetiapine 25–50 mg. The cardiac stack warrants a fresh ECG before initiation. The Association of Medicine and Psychiatry consensus (Xiong 2020) recommends baseline ECG when ≥2 QT risk factors are present. Dad has at least 4: age >65, bradycardia (HR 49–53), conduction abnormalities (LAFB, borderline 1° AVB, QRS 120), and polypharmacy with QT-active agents (duloxetine, doxepin, trazodone PRN). The last ECG (5/19/2025) showed QTc 401 — within range — but it's a year old. Quetiapine carries a pharmacovigilance signal for QT prolongation and TdP (FAERS ROR 1.58/2.39), almost always in the presence of multiple risk factors. Cost of a fresh ECG is low; cost of layering quetiapine on a year-old baseline is real.
The norquetiapine NRI concern is smaller than worried. Norquetiapine is a potent NET inhibitor (Ki 12 nM) and the metabolite-mediated noradrenergic activity is part of why quetiapine was initially feared as an analog for the mirtazapine mechanism. But the PET data (Nyberg 2013) show only 19% NET occupancy at 150 mg/day and 35% at 300 mg/day; at 25–50 mg the metabolite exposure is far below the threshold for meaningful NET occupancy. Quetiapine 25–50 mg is unlikely to recapitulate the mirtazapine-at-45 mg activation mechanism, despite the metabolite's pharmacology. The doxepin reduction alone may be most of the answer. Last night's episode happened after a same-evening uptitration of doxepin from 6 → 12 mg HS. TCAs in older adults can produce paradoxical activation, restlessness, and physical symptoms during rapid uptitration. Dr. R's first move — taking doxepin back to 6 mg — directly addresses that mechanism. Whether last night repeats at 6 mg is the question the next 1–2 nights would answer, and that observation is destroyed if a second new agent is added concurrently. The handout #08 framing applies here: don't run two pharmacological changes simultaneously in a 76-year-old when one of them addresses the most plausible cause directly.

Tier 1 — Receptor Pharmacology of Low-Dose Quetiapine ★★★★★
Sato H, Ito C, Hiraoka K et al. Histamine H1 Receptor Occupancy by the New-Generation Antipsychotics Olanzapine and Quetiapine: A PET Study in Healthy Volunteers.
Psychopharmacology 2015;232:3497–3505. Read here.
Key findingA single 25 mg dose of quetiapine produced 56–81% H1 receptor occupancy in the cerebral cortex — high enough to drive subjective sedation, consistent with the binding hierarchy where H1 affinity (Ki 4.4 nM) far exceeds D2 affinity (Ki 626 nM).
How it appliesAnchors Dr. R's "acts differently" pharmacology claim. At 25 mg, quetiapine is functionally an antihistamine first and a D2 antagonist a distant fourth.
Lako IM, van den Heuvel ER, Knegtering H, Bruggeman R, Taxis K. Estimating Dopamine D2 Receptor Occupancy for Doses of 8 Antipsychotics: A Meta-Analysis.
J Clin Psychopharmacol 2013;33:675–681. Read here.
Key findingQuetiapine's maximum achievable D2 occupancy is only 49.1% (95% CI 18.7–79.6), far below the 65% EPS threshold. By contrast, olanzapine and the D2 partial agonists routinely reach 65–80% at clinical doses.
How it appliesThe mechanism-distinct-from-prior-agents argument. The receptors that produced EPS on aripiprazole/brexpiprazole/olanzapine for Dad are not the dominant receptors at quetiapine 25–50 mg.
Nyberg S, Jucaite A, Takano A et al. Norepinephrine Transporter Occupancy in the Human Brain After Oral Administration of Quetiapine XR.
Int J Neuropsychopharmacol 2013;16:2235–2244. Read here.
Key findingNorquetiapine (the active NRI metabolite) produced only 19% NET occupancy at quetiapine XR 150 mg/day and 35% at 300 mg/day. The threshold for meaningful NET occupancy requires parent-drug doses well above 50 mg.
How it appliesThe mirtazapine-analog concern via the noradrenergic metabolite is smaller than initially worried at 25–50 mg parent dose. The metabolite-mediated activation mechanism is not meaningfully recruited at this dose.
Tier 2 — Quetiapine and Ketamine Interaction ★★★
Veraart JKE, Smith-Apeldoorn SY, Bakker IM et al. Pharmacodynamic Interactions Between Ketamine and Psychiatric Medications Used in the Treatment of Depression: A Systematic Review.
Int J Neuropsychopharmacol 2021;24:808–831. Read here.
Key findingBenzodiazepines repeatedly reduced the duration of ketamine's antidepressant effect (Andrashko 2020; Feeney 2022; dose-dependent above 8 mg diazepam equivalent). Antipsychotic data are sparser: risperidone attenuated ketamine-induced brain perfusion changes, clozapine blunted positive symptoms, haloperidol showed an interaction. Quetiapine specifically: no published data.
How it appliesThe benzo mechanism (GABA-A potentiation) doesn't translate to quetiapine, but the 5-HT2A antagonism is on the same downstream pathway ketamine uses for plasticity. Direction of prior is "attenuation or neutral," not "augmentation." Loops back to why the ketamine clinic's input matters before tomorrow's session.
Amiaz R, Saporta R, Noy A, Berkenstadt H, Weiser M. Can Quetiapine Prolong the Antidepressant Effect of Ketamine?: A 5-Year Follow-Up Study.
J Clin Psychopharmacol 2021;41:673–675. Read here.
Key findingSmall 5-year follow-up (N = 16) found that patients taking quetiapine had significantly longer time to ketamine relapse (966 vs. 81 days), with 3 of 5 quetiapine patients maintaining remission at 5 years. Hypothesis-generating; very low-quality evidence.
How it appliesThe one data point pointing in the augment-rather-than-attenuate direction. N = 16 with no control for selection effects. Worth knowing exists but not strong enough to override the mechanistic prior or the absence of trial data.
