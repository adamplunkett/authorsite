Differential — in rough order of weight

  1. Doxepin 12 mg-related (and ongoing nordoxepin) — temporally most proximate. Doxepin at 12 mg moves from pure-H1 territory (3–6 mg) toward anticholinergic and 5-HT2A effects; in an elderly patient with prodromal Lewy-spectrum vulnerability, this could plausibly produce or unmask myoclonic/jerky movements. Nordoxepin's 31-hour half-life means residual effects persist for ~5 days post-discontinuation. Doxepin discontinued ~5/21; nordoxepin likely meaningfully present through ~5/26.
  2. Prodromal Lewy-spectrum motor symptoms unmasked by doxepin — Dad's underlying vulnerability (formal RBD 8.5 years, gait abnormality, balance findings) provides a substrate that pharmacological insults can unmask. PLMD (periodic limb movement disorder) is common in prodromal synucleinopathy.
  3. SNRI-related (Cymbalta steady state or taper effects) — SSRIs/SNRIs are documented to exacerbate RLS/PLMD. Taper attempts on 5/12 and 5/21 may have produced acute withdrawal contributions; Cymbalta steady state may baseline-worsen any underlying motor signal.
  4. Sleep-deprivation myoclonus — extreme sleep deprivation independently produces jerky movements.
  5. Residual drug-induced parkinsonism / motor signal — from recent olanzapine, brexpiprazole, lithium, pramipexole exposures (March–April). These can take weeks to months to fully resolve.
  6. Acute anxiety tremor — possible but typically more diffuse than focal leg/foot jerks.
