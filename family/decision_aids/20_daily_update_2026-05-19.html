Concerns about the proposed plan
Cross-referenced to prior research where relevant.
  • The Cymbalta → Lexapro switch contradicts the stated goal of reducing acute pre-session anxiety. Duloxetine discontinuation produces acute anxiety, dizziness, paresthesias, nausea, agitation — introducing acute anxiety into the window Dr. Chilton wants quieted. Lexapro start carries activation risk in the first 2–4 weeks, especially in older adults; Dad's activation-intolerance pattern across aripiprazole, mirtazapine, brexpiprazole, olanzapine, pramipexole, and lithium makes him a worse-than-average candidate. SSRI anxiolytic effect takes 4–8 weeks — even with the A2 gap, the turbulence window lands before any benefit window does.
  • The switch goes backward in Dad's treatment history. Escitalopram was the long-running SSRI through ~10/2025 and was replaced by duloxetine because the SSRI wasn't holding the depression. No new evidence that it would work better now than it did 7 months ago. Worth reviewing alongside handout #09 — Antidepressant three-arm decision aid.
  • Removing trazodone and doxepin is the wrong direction. Both are AGS-Beers-endorsed in older adults at these doses, Chilton-cleared, and Dad-tolerated. See handout #13. Lemborexant alone is reasonable — it's what Julane endorsed and the DORA-class add handout #19 flagged as the next sleep step. Lemborexant as replacement for the working stack is a different and worse proposal than lemborexant as an addition.
  • Divalproex has no clear stated indication. Costs in older adults: cognitive effects, sedation, weight gain (in someone already losing weight unintentionally), hepatotoxicity, thrombocytopenia. Dad already filled it once and didn't take it. Lithium was discontinued 4/21 because the mood-stabilizer trial didn't pay off. See handout #01 — Depakote.
  • The post-session coherence is a real ketamine response signal, not a footnote. "Felt better in the chair" and "had an antidepressant response" are different variables. Ketamine response often shows up over hours to days, not in the chair. Dad's specific IV-responder status (Feb 2026) strengthens the case for getting back to IV at A2 rather than backing off the modality.
  • "Retraumatizing" imports a causal claim about durable psychological harm that cannot be made in the immediate aftermath of a difficult dissociative experience. Hard ≠ harmful. The post-session coherence is direct evidence against the "actively reinforcing prior trauma" reading.
  • Julane having less voice on meds is structurally backward. She has continuous longitudinal observation of Dad in functional state, no relationship-management pressure to maintain access, and no prior medication decisions to defend. The proposal pattern recognized in the Seroquel episode (senior provider names a clinical goal → Dr. R assembles an aggressive restructuring in the name of that goal) is exactly why a third perpendicular voice matters.
  • ECT belongs on the table as a contingent backup if a fairly-tested ketamine course doesn't respond, not as a pivot driven by one rough Day 1 IV session at an unfamiliar setting. See handout #04 — Ketamine decision aid for prior framing.
