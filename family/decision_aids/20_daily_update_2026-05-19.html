Concerns about the proposed plan
Cross-referenced to prior research where relevant.
  • The Cymbalta → Lexapro switch contradicts the stated goal of reducing acute pre-session anxiety. Duloxetine discontinuation produces acute anxiety, dizziness, paresthesias, nausea, agitation. Lexapro start carries activation risk in the first 2–4 weeks, especially in older adults; Dad's activation-intolerance pattern makes him a worse-than-average candidate. SSRI anxiolytic effect takes 4–8 weeks — even with the June 8 start, the turbulence window lands before any benefit window.
  • Chilton's "SNRI is riskier than SSRI as a ketamine background" framing is not the standard read. Del Casale 2025 (JAMA Psychiatry, n=55,480) found the opposite at population scale: SNRI + esketamine outperformed SSRI + esketamine on mortality (5.3% vs 9.1%), hospitalization, and relapse. Dad's documented prior IV response was specifically on duloxetine. Worth asking Chilton what she's basing the framing on. (Queued as OE re-evaluation.) See also handout #06, which will be revisited.
  • Julane's preferred sequencing is structurally more conservative. Run the first 1–2 ketamine sessions on the existing stack, observe response and tolerability, then revisit any antidepressant switch with that data in hand. Advantage: doesn't introduce two pharmacological changes (SSRI switch + new modality) in the same window; preserves the duloxetine background the documented Feb 2026 response was on.
  • Removing trazodone and doxepin is the wrong direction. Both are AGS-Beers-endorsed at these doses, Chilton-cleared, Dad-tolerated. See handout #13. Lemborexant alone (Julane-endorsed) is reasonable as an addition per handout #19; coupling it to removal of the working stack is a different and worse proposal.
  • Divalproex is the most ambiguous element of the plan. Chilton's role is unclear: recommended, or merely noted on the chart. Dad's memory of the call is hesitant. Even if recommended, the indication isn't stated. Divalproex in older adults brings cognitive effects, sedation, weight gain (in someone already losing weight), hepatotoxicity, thrombocytopenia, and — most relevantly for Dad — a documented drug-induced parkinsonism signal that overlaps with the Lewy-spectrum question the neurology workup is meant to address. See handout #01. If divalproex does end up being pursued, the absolute family-side requirement is to surface any new motor or cognitive symptoms early rather than mask or minimize them, so an unrecognized drug-induced parkinsonism doesn't get attributed to disease progression. (Queued as OE re-evaluation.)
  • The post-session coherence on 5/18 was a real ketamine response signal, not a footnote. Dad's specific IV-responder status (Feb 2026) reinforces it. The session was destabilizing in the chair; the post-session functional state is a separate variable.
  • The Feb 2026 context vs. the 5/18 context is informative. Feb: family present, sense of support, dramatic response. 5/18: Royal Oak, no in-room provider, "Africa" framing, didn't feel attached to anyone. Set and setting shape both experience and antidepressant response. The A2 transfer is the practical lever.
