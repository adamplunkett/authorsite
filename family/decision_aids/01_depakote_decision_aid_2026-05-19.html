Recommendation

Defer divalproex. The 5/19 OpenEvidence consultation (#11) reinforces and extends the original 5/13 case against: no published RCT evidence for unipolar TRD augmentation, not in any major guideline for this indication, ranked at or near the bottom of OE's geriatric TRD augmentation hierarchy, and a documented drug-induced parkinsonism signal that is particularly dangerous in Dad's specific prodromal-synucleinopathy profile (UK Biobank data show persistent valproate–PD association even after excluding prescriptions within 5 years of diagnosis, suggesting valproate can unmask subclinical disease). Chilton's role in raising divalproex is ambiguous (recommended? or simply noted on chart?) and should be clarified in writing before any move. If "do more than ketamine" pressure persists, bupropion augmentation (handout #21) is a substantially stronger evidence-based alternative — direct OPTIMUM evidence (28.2% remission), no parkinsonism risk, more favorable cardiac and interaction profile, and preserves the duloxetine background that backed Dad's documented February 2026 ketamine response. If divalproex is nonetheless pursued, the absolute family-side requirement is a formal monitoring protocol (motor, cognitive, ammonia/CBC/LFT) with explicit discontinuation triggers known to all family members upfront so any new symptom is surfaced rather than minimized.

The evidence (revised 2026-05-19 with OE #11 findings)
What might support starting Depakote What argues against — in Dad's specific profile
Single small open-label study (Ghabrash 2016, n=14, ages 19–59, no control group) showed some MADRS improvement when divalproex was added to AD. This is the only published study suggesting a unipolar TRD augmentation benefit. Does not apply to Dad's age group or dose; no controlled trial replication. No RCT evidence for divalproex augmentation in unipolar TRD at any age. OE #11 confirms: not in any major guideline for this indication (APA, NICE, CANMAT, Maudsley, AAGP, VA/DoD). The Schaff 1993 retrospective (n=63) reclassified 12 of the "unipolar" patients as covert cyclers — meaning even the favorable old data is partially bipolar.
Widely prescribed off-label at low doses, generally short-term tolerated in adults without specific neurological vulnerabilities. Weak evidence: signals only that the drug isn't acutely toxic at this exposure level. Does not address benefit for unipolar TRD or transfer to Dad's specific profile. OPTIMUM hierarchy excludes divalproex. The largest pragmatic geriatric TRD augmentation RCT (Lenze 2023, n=619) ranked aripiprazole augmentation (28.9% remission), bupropion augmentation (28.2%), and lithium augmentation step 2 (18.9%). Divalproex was not in any comparative arm. OE #11: "Divalproex is not mentioned in any major guideline or review for unipolar TRD augmentation in older adults."
— Subtherapeutic dose doesn't protect against parkinsonism. Brugger 2016 critical review of 116 published cases of valproate-induced parkinsonism: in 113 of 116, plasma concentrations were at or below the therapeutic range when parkinsonism emerged. Dose-independent risk.
— UK Biobank data: valproate can unmask subclinical Parkinson's. Belete 2023 (1,433 PD cases, 8,598 controls) — sodium valproate was robustly associated with incident PD, association persisted after adjusting for epilepsy and after excluding prescriptions within 5 years of PD diagnosis. The temporal gap matters: it suggests valproate accelerates or unmasks neurodegeneration rather than merely co-occurring with PD.
— Time to onset of valproate parkinsonism is insidious. Mean 75.7 months in one series, but shorter latencies reported. Recovery after discontinuation is often prolonged (mean 4.5 months, range days to 2 years) and sometimes incomplete. Some patients who appeared to improve later developed idiopathic PD — consistent with unmasking rather than purely drug-induced.
— Dad's profile is the highest-risk stratum for unmasking. Per Arnaldi 2021, age >70 + constipation + putaminal dopaminergic dysfunction = HR 5.71 combined for phenoconversion. Dad has two of three criteria already (DAT-SPECT pending). Introducing a drug known to unmask subclinical dopaminergic degeneration in this profile carries qualitatively different risk than in a general elderly population.
— Brain volume / cognitive risk. Tariot 2011 trial in older adults with early Alzheimer's at ~250 mg/day: faster brain volume loss on MRI, worse MMSE over 12 months than placebo. Alsukhni 2023 review documents reversible cognitive decline mimicking dementia (mean latency ~6.87 years; most cases had parkinsonian features; all recovered on discontinuation). In a patient already reporting brain fog and cognitive concerns, drug-attributed cognitive change would be difficult to distinguish from disease progression.
— Confounds the pending U-M Movement Disorders evaluation. Any new motor or cognitive finding during a divalproex trial cannot be cleanly attributed — drug effect vs disease progression are indistinguishable. The neurology workup is specifically designed to characterize the prodromal-synucleinopathy question; divalproex would compromise it.
— Confounds the B12 / methylmalonic acidemia follow-up. Divalproex disrupts folate and B12 processing. Dad's MMA recheck on the 8/6/2026 follow-up would be uninterpretable on a divalproex background.
— Possible attenuation of ketamine response. Xu 2015 (n=36, bipolar): patients on valproate maintenance showed smaller ketamine antidepressant effect size (d = 0.79) than lithium-maintained patients (d = 2.27); not statistically significant in the small sample but directionally concerning. Mechanism: valproate's GABAergic enhancement could theoretically dampen ketamine's glutamatergic mechanism. Relevant given the upcoming June 8 IV ketamine restart.
— Significant interaction with doxepin. Unterecker 2014: valproate increases doxepin + N-doxepin serum levels 2.2× via UGT inhibition (p < 0.001). Clinically relevant given Dad's current doxepin 6 mg HS.
— Activation-intolerance pattern. Dad has documented adverse responses to six prior agents across mechanistically distinct pathways (aripiprazole, mirtazapine, brexpiprazole, olanzapine, pramipexole, lithium). OE #11 noted that while divalproex operates through a distinct mechanism (GABA enhancement, HDAC inhibition, sodium channel), the breadth of prior intolerance is itself an independent clinical caution.
— Cochrane review of valproate for agitation in dementia (including Lewy body): no more effective than placebo and associated with more adverse effects including falls, gait disturbances, sedation, and tremor (Baillon 2018).
— Bupropion augmentation is the much stronger alternative if "do more than ketamine" pressure persists. Direct OPTIMUM evidence (28.2% remission), mechanistically pro-dopaminergic (does not produce parkinsonism), favorable cardiac profile, compatible with ketamine. See handout #21.
Evidence strength against starting Depakote: ★★★★★
Convergence of guideline absence, no RCT support for unipolar TRD, mechanistic parkinsonism-unmasking signal directly relevant to Dad's highest-risk profile, multiple confounders for the active workup (neurology, MMA, ketamine response), interaction with current doxepin, and the existence of a substantially stronger evidence-based alternative (bupropion augmentation).
